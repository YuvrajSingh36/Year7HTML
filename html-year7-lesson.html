📖 The Concept

What Exactly is HTML?

HTML stands for HyperText Markup Language. Think of it as the skeleton of a webpage — it gives the page its structure, just like bones give your body its shape.
🦴
Skeleton of the page
HTML gives structure & meaning to content
🏷️
Made of Tags (Labels)
Tags tell the browser what each piece of content is
📁
Saved as .html files
Any browser can open & display them
<HTML>
📄 Head — Page Info
👀 Body — What You See
</HTML>
Every webpage follows this structure
🥪 Phase 1 — I Do

Tags Are Like a Sandwich

Every HTML tag has two parts that wrap around content — just like bread wraps around a filling.

🍞 Opening Tag   <h1>
🥬 Content   — What the visitor sees
🍞 Closing Tag   </h1>   (the / means "stop!")
🔑 The Golden Rule
Every opening tag <tag> needs a matching closing tag </tag>

<h1>My Favorite Hobby</h1>
<p>I love playing football!</p>

🧠 Key Concept

Head vs Body — What Goes Where?

<head> — The Brain (Hidden)

Information about the page. Visitors can't see this — but the browser needs it.

<title> — Tab name in browser
<meta> — Settings & info
👁️ Invisible to visitors
<body> — The Body (Visible!)

Everything visitors actually see on the page goes here.

<h1> <h2> Headings
<p> Paragraphs of text
<h3> <b> <i> Formatting
👀 Visible to visitors
💡 Memory trick: Head = secrets the browser keeps. Body = what you show the world!
🤝 Phase 2 — We Do

The HTML Skeleton — Copy This!

<!-- Every HTML file starts with this --> <!DOCTYPE html> <html> <head> <title>My First Page</title> </head> <body> <h1>Welcome to [Your Name]'s World!</h1> <p>This is my first website. I am learning how to code!</p> <h2>My Favourite Thing: [Hobby]</h2> <p>I really like [Hobby] because it is fun.</p> </body> </html>
✏️ Change the highlighted parts to be about YOU
<!DOCTYPE html>
Tells the browser: "This is an HTML file"
<title>
Name shown in the browser tab — only in <head>
<h1>
Biggest heading — like a chapter title
<h2>
Sub-heading — slightly smaller than <h1>
<p>
A paragraph of normal text
